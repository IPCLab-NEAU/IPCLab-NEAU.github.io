Intelligent Perception and Computing Laboratory
The Intelligent Perception and Computing Laboratory is part of the Smart Livestock Innovation Team under the College of Electrical Engineering and Information, Northeast Agricultural University. The laboratory is dedicated to enhancing the physiological perception of livestock animals such as beef cattle, dairy cows, and pigs through cutting-edge computer vision technologies. The laboratory focuses on developing innovative intelligent monitoring systems that provide precise perception and analysis of key physiological parameters, including weight, body size, and behavior. By integrating deep learning with visual perception technologies, our goal is to achieve more accurate, efficient, and contactless animal health monitoring. This will offer intelligent solutions for modern agriculture, advancing digitalization and precision management in the industry. In our research, we not only emphasize technological innovation but also aim to apply our findings to practical livestock farming. Through intelligent methods, we optimize the animal breeding process, enhancing farming efficiency and improving animal welfare.
